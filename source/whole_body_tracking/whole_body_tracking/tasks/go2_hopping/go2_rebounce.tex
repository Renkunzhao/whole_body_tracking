\section{Current Task}

The current task is continuous rebounce control for Go2. At reset, each
environment samples a scalar height command
\begin{equation}
    h^{*} \sim \mathcal{U}(0.5, 0.8)\ \mathrm{m},
\end{equation}
then places the robot base at the corresponding height with zero root velocity
and the default joint state. The policy must keep rebounding for the full
episode. The objective is not to jump higher forever, but to make each rebound
apex return close to the reset/drop height while maintaining a flat quadruped
posture.

Unlike the earlier single-rebounce setup, reaching an apex is no longer a
termination condition. The episode continues until either a failure condition is
met or the $20$ s time limit is reached. A successful rollout should therefore
show repeated cycles of
\begin{center}
    fall,\quad support interaction,\quad rebound,\quad airborne apex,\quad
    re-arm after the feet return near the support.
\end{center}

\section{Environment Variants}

Two environment IDs are used:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\hline
ID & Scene & Notes \\
\hline
\texttt{Go2-Rebounce-Flat} & rigid plane & 4096 envs, env spacing $2.5$ m \\
\texttt{Go2-Rebounce-Trampoline} & deformable trampoline & 2048 envs, env spacing $4.0$ m \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The trampoline variant keeps the same rebounce MDP terms but replaces the
support with a deformable trampoline and repins the trampoline rim. Task success
does not rely on rigid-deformable contact sensors. Apex and foot-clearance gates
use only root state and kinematic foot body positions.

\section{Timing}

The simulator time step and action decimation are
\begin{equation}
    \Delta t_{\mathrm{sim}} = 0.005\ \mathrm{s},
    \qquad
    d = 4,
    \qquad
    \Delta t = d\Delta t_{\mathrm{sim}} = 0.02\ \mathrm{s}.
\end{equation}
The current training episode length is
\begin{equation}
    T_{\mathrm{episode}} = 20\ \mathrm{s}.
\end{equation}
IsaacLab multiplies every weighted reward term by $\Delta t$, and logged
\texttt{Episode\_Reward/*} values are additionally divided by
$T_{\mathrm{episode}}$.

\section{Reset and Command}

The rebounce command is
\begin{equation}
    \bm{c} = [h^{*}].
\end{equation}
In the current baseline, the reset height and the target rebound-apex height are
the same scalar. The reset event writes $h^{*}$ into the command buffer and sets
the root state to
\begin{equation}
    \bm{p}_{\mathrm{root}} =
    \bm{o}_{\mathrm{env}} +
    \left[p_x^0,\ p_y^0,\ h^{*}+z_{\mathrm{offset}}\right],
    \qquad
    \bm{v}_{\mathrm{root}}=\bm{0},
\end{equation}
where $z_{\mathrm{offset}}=0$ in the current config. Joint positions are reset to
the default Go2 pose and joint velocities are reset to zero.

The command term stores the following buffers:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\hline
Symbol & Buffer & Meaning \\
\hline
$h^{*}$ & \texttt{peak\_height} & commanded target height \\
$z_0$ & \texttt{drop\_z} & root height latched after reset \\
$z_{\max}$ & \texttt{peak\_z} & running root max during rebound ascent \\
$b_{\downarrow}$ & \texttt{has\_descended} & root has had negative vertical velocity \\
$b_{\uparrow}$ & \texttt{has\_rebounded} & root has had positive velocity after descent \\
\texttt{armed} & \texttt{\_apex\_armed} & next apex can be counted \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{Observations and Actions}

The actor observation is
\begin{equation}
    \bm{o}^{\pi}_t =
    \left[
        h^{*},\,
        \bm{\omega}^{b}_t,\,
        \bm{g}^{b}_t,\,
        \tilde{\bm{q}}_t,\,
        \tilde{\dot{\bm{q}}}_t,\,
        \bm{a}_{t-1}
    \right].
\end{equation}
The critic additionally observes world root position and base linear velocity.
The policy action is a 12-DoF joint-position target action with the default Go2
joint offset. In the trampoline variant, a separate action term keeps the
trampoline rim pinned.

\section{Apex Event and Metrics}

An airborne apex is detected by a root vertical velocity sign change gated by a
geometric foot-clearance condition:
\begin{equation}
    b^{\mathrm{raw}}_t =
    b_{\downarrow,t}
    \wedge b_{\uparrow,t}
    \wedge (\dot{z}_{t-1} > 0)
    \wedge (\dot{z}_{t} \le 0),
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    b^{\mathrm{air}}_t =
    \mathbb{I}
    \left[
        \min_{i\in\mathcal{F}} z^{\mathrm{local}}_{i,t}
        > z_{\mathrm{surface}} + 0.08
    \right],
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{F}$ is the set of four Go2 feet and $z_{\mathrm{surface}}=0$ in
the local support frame.

To avoid counting multiple vertical velocity zero-crossings around the same
physical apex, the apex detector is armed only once per bounce. After an
airborne apex is counted, it is disarmed. It is re-armed only when the feet
return near the support:
\begin{equation}
    \min_{i\in\mathcal{F}} z^{\mathrm{local}}_{i,t}
    \le z_{\mathrm{surface}} + 0.08.
\end{equation}
Thus an apex counted by the metric/reward/timeout logic is
\begin{equation}
    b^{\mathrm{apex}}_t =
    \texttt{armed}_t
    \wedge b^{\mathrm{raw}}_t
    \wedge b^{\mathrm{air}}_t.
\end{equation}

The logged count metrics are:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{0.28\linewidth}p{0.30\linewidth}p{0.30\linewidth}}
\hline
Metric & Condition & Interpretation \\
\hline
\texttt{apex\_count} & $b^{\mathrm{apex}}_t$ & physical airborne apex count \\
\texttt{target\_apex\_count} &
$b^{\mathrm{apex}}_t \wedge |z_t-z_0|<0.25$ &
apex count close to target height \\
\texttt{error\_peak\_height} &
mean $|z_t-z_0|$ over counted apexes &
height tracking error \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{Termination}

The current termination terms are:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{0.35\linewidth}p{0.28\linewidth}p{0.27\linewidth}}
\hline
Term & Condition & Role \\
\hline
\texttt{base\_orientation} & base tilt angle $>0.6$ rad & failure \\
\texttt{non\_foot\_contact} & non-foot rigid contact $>1$ N & failure \\
\texttt{no\_airborne\_apex\_timeout} & no airborne apex for $2$ s & failure \\
\texttt{time\_out} & $20$ s reached & successful long rollout if other failures absent \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\texttt{no\_airborne\_apex\_timeout} uses the same re-armed airborne-apex logic as
the metric and the apex reward. This prevents a standing policy from simply
waiting for the long time limit.

\section{Rewards}

The weighted reward is
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
    R_t = \Delta t \Big(
        &-250\,r^{\mathrm{fail}}_t
        -250\,r^{\mathrm{timeout}}_t
        +5\,r^{\mathrm{progress}}_t
        +50\,r^{\mathrm{apex}}_t \\
        &-2\,r^{\mathrm{orient}}_t
        -10^{-2}\,r^{\mathrm{action}}_t
        -0.05\,r^{\mathrm{joint}}_t
        -10\,r^{\mathrm{limit}}_t
    \Big).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}

The failure pulse is
\begin{equation}
    r^{\mathrm{fail}}_t =
    \mathbb{I}[\texttt{base\_orientation}]
    + \mathbb{I}[\texttt{non\_foot\_contact}]
    + \mathbb{I}[\texttt{no\_airborne\_apex\_timeout}].
\end{equation}
The timeout penalty is conditional:
\begin{equation}
    r^{\mathrm{timeout}}_t =
    \mathbb{I}[\texttt{time\_out}]
    \mathbb{I}[\texttt{apex\_count}<1].
\end{equation}
Thus a policy that reaches the normal time limit after producing apexes is not
penalized by \texttt{failed\_timeout}.

Let
\begin{equation}
    e^z_t = |z^{w}_{\mathrm{root},t} - z_0|.
\end{equation}
The flat-body gate is
\begin{equation}
    g^{\mathrm{flat}}_t =
    \exp\left(
        -\frac{\left\lVert \bm{g}^{b}_{t,xy}\right\rVert^2}{0.35^2}
    \right),
\end{equation}
and the soft foot-clearance gate is
\begin{equation}
    g^{\mathrm{feet}}_t =
    \sigma\left(
        \frac{
            \min_{i\in\mathcal{F}} z^{\mathrm{local}}_{i,t}
            -(z_{\mathrm{surface}}+0.08)
        }{0.015}
    \right).
\end{equation}

The dense rebound shaping term is
\begin{equation}
    r^{\mathrm{progress}}_t =
    \mathbb{I}[b_{\uparrow,t}]
    \exp\left(-\left(\frac{e^z_t}{0.25}\right)^2\right)
    g^{\mathrm{flat}}_t
    g^{\mathrm{feet}}_t.
\end{equation}
The apex pulse reward is
\begin{equation}
    r^{\mathrm{apex}}_t =
    \mathbb{I}[b^{\mathrm{apex}}_t]
    \exp\left(-\left(\frac{e^z_t}{0.10}\right)^2\right)
    g^{\mathrm{flat}}_t
    g^{\mathrm{feet}}_t.
\end{equation}
The remaining penalties are standard orientation, action-rate, joint-deviation,
and joint-position-limit regularizers.

\paragraph{Reward scale.}
In the current $20$ s setup, a single perfect apex pulse contributes
\[
    50 \cdot 0.02 / 20 = 0.05
\]
to the logged \texttt{Episode\_Reward/rebounce\_height}. A single failure
termination contributes approximately
\[
    -250 \cdot 0.02 / 20 = -0.25
\]
for the environments where that failure happened, before averaging over all
reset environments.

\section{Experiment Roadmap}

The experiment order should separate task-definition changes from style,
efficiency, and final ablations.

\subsection{Step 0: Freeze the Current Baseline}

First train and save a clean baseline with the current config. Record the git
state, run ID, checkpoint, and videos. The expected healthy indicators are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \texttt{Episode\_Termination/time\_out} close to $1$,
    \item \texttt{no\_airborne\_apex\_timeout} close to $0$,
    \item \texttt{apex\_count} close to the visually observed bounce count,
    \item \texttt{target\_apex\_count}/\texttt{apex\_count} high,
    \item \texttt{error\_peak\_height} low.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Step 1: Decouple Initial Drop Height from Target Apex Height}

The current baseline uses
\[
    h_{\mathrm{drop}} = h^{*}.
\]
The first task-definition experiment should decouple them:
\[
    h^{*} \sim \mathcal{U}(0.5,0.8), \qquad
    h_{\mathrm{drop}} = h^{*} + \epsilon.
\]
Start with a small offset
\[
    \epsilon \sim \mathcal{U}(-0.1,0.1)\ \mathrm{m},
\]
then increase the range or sample the two heights independently if the policy
remains stable. This tests whether the policy controls rebound height, rather
than only recovering to the height from which it was dropped.

\subsection{Step 2: Add In-Place Hopping}

After the height task is fixed, add penalties or tracking terms for horizontal
and yaw drift. Suggested diagnostics are
\[
    \lVert \bm{p}_{xy,t}-\bm{p}_{xy,0}\rVert,\qquad
    \lVert \bm{v}_{xy,t}\rVert,\qquad
    |\psi_t-\psi_0|,\qquad
    |\dot{\psi}_t|.
\]
Start with small weights so vertical rebounce quality is not suppressed. The
goal is to keep the robot rebounding in place without sacrificing apex-height
tracking.

\subsection{Step 3: Regulate Leg Motion in Flight}

The current policy can show fast leg motion in the air. Test the following
regularization variants one at a time:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item increase \texttt{joint\_deviation\_l1} weight, e.g.
    $-0.05 \rightarrow -0.10 \rightarrow -0.20$;
    \item add a joint-velocity penalty, starting from a small weight;
    \item reduce or reshape the foot-clearance gate to check whether the apex
    reward encourages spreading or lifting the feet near the apex.
\end{enumerate}
This step should be done before energy optimization so the source of the flight
leg motion is identifiable.

\subsection{Step 4: Add Energy Optimization}

Only after the behavior is stable and visually acceptable, add energy terms.
Candidate penalties are
\[
    \sum_j \tau_{j,t}^{2}
    \qquad\text{or}\qquad
    \sum_j |\tau_{j,t}\dot{q}_{j,t}|.
\]
Use small weights and monitor \texttt{target\_apex\_count},
\texttt{error\_peak\_height}, and \texttt{time\_out}. If energy decreases but
height tracking or survival degrades, the weight is too large.

\subsection{Step 5: Final Ablation Experiments}

Run formal ablations after the final task definition is stable. Suggested
variants are:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{0.22\linewidth}p{0.34\linewidth}p{0.34\linewidth}}
\hline
Variant & Change & Main question \\
\hline
Full & final setup & reference \\
No apex timeout & remove \texttt{no\_airborne\_apex\_timeout} & is the anti-wait gate needed? \\
No progress reward & set \texttt{rebound\_progress}=0 & is dense shaping needed? \\
No foot gate & remove foot-clearance gates & does it prevent standing or foot exploits? \\
No in-place reward & remove $xy/yaw$ terms & does the policy drift? \\
No regulation & remove flight-leg regulation & does leg motion return? \\
No energy reward & remove energy term & efficiency versus performance \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

For every variant, compare success rate, failure distribution, height error,
apex count, target-apex count, drift metrics, energy, and videos.

\section{Recommended Order}

The recommended order is:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item freeze the current continuous-rebounce baseline;
    \item decouple drop height and target height;
    \item add in-place constraints;
    \item regulate flight leg motion;
    \item add energy optimization;
    \item run final ablations.
\end{enumerate}

This order first fixes the task definition, then the motion style, then
efficiency, and only then performs explanatory ablations.
